# Cyber Resilience and Redundancy

## Objectives
- Understand how organizations continue operating during disruption
- Explain redundancy, backups, and recovery objectives
- Connect resilience to availability and business continuity

## Cyber Resilience
- **Cyber Resilience:** The ability to continue operating, respond, recover, and adapt after a cyber incident or outage
- Resilience focuses on keeping critical services available and reducing downtime

## Redundancy
- **Redundancy:** Duplicate systems, components, or paths used to prevent a single point of failure
- Examples:
  - Multiple power supplies
  - UPS and generator
  - RAID storage
  - Load balancer
  - Failover server
  - Multiple network links
  - Secondary DNS
  - Cloud availability zones

## Backup
- **Backup:** A copy of data used for recovery
- Backups should be encrypted, protected, monitored, and tested
- A backup is only useful if it can be restored successfully

## Recovery Terms
- **RTO:** Recovery Time Objective; maximum acceptable time to restore a service
- **RPO:** Recovery Point Objective; maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time
- **MTTR:** Mean Time To Repair
- **MTBF:** Mean Time Between Failures

## Site Types
- **Hot Site:** Fully ready environment with systems and data available quickly
- **Warm Site:** Partially ready environment that needs some setup
- **Cold Site:** Basic facility with little or no active equipment

## Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery
- **Business Continuity:** Keeps essential business functions operating
- **Disaster Recovery:** Restores technology systems after disruption

## Key Takeaway
- Redundancy helps prevent downtime; backups and recovery plans help restore operations after failure.
