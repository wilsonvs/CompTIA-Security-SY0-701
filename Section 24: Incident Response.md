# Incident Response

## Objectives

- Understand the incident response lifecycle.
- Explain roles, evidence handling, communication, and containment.
- Connect incident response to SOC workflows and documentation.

## Lifecycle

A common incident response lifecycle includes:

1. Preparation
2. Detection and analysis
3. Containment
4. Eradication
5. Recovery
6. Lessons learned

## Preparation

Preparation includes playbooks, contact lists, logging, tools, access, backups, tabletop exercises, and communication plans. Strong preparation reduces confusion during an incident.

## Detection and Analysis

During analysis, the team determines what happened, which assets are affected, whether the incident is active, and what evidence supports the conclusion.

Important questions:

- What triggered the incident?
- Which systems and users are involved?
- What is the timeline?
- What data may be affected?
- Is there active attacker access?
- What containment is needed?

## Containment, Eradication, Recovery

Containment limits damage. Eradication removes the cause. Recovery restores normal operations while monitoring for recurrence.

Examples:

- Disable account
- Isolate endpoint
- Block indicator
- Remove malware
- Patch vulnerability
- Restore from backup
- Monitor for repeated activity

## Lessons Learned

After the incident, document root cause, timeline, impact, response actions, control gaps, and improvements.
