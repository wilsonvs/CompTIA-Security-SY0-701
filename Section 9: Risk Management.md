# Risk Management

## Objectives
- Understand how organizations identify, measure, and handle risk
- Explain the relationship between threats, vulnerabilities, assets, likelihood, and impact
- Compare common risk response strategies

## Risk Management
- **Risk Management:** The process of identifying, analyzing, prioritizing, and responding to risk
- **Risk:** The possibility that a threat may exploit a vulnerability and cause harm to an asset or organization
- **Asset:** Anything valuable that needs protection, such as systems, data, users, applications, facilities, or business processes
- **Threat:** Anything that can cause harm, damage, loss, or compromise
- **Vulnerability:** A weakness that can be exploited by a threat

## Risk Formula
- Risk is commonly understood as the relationship between:
  - **Threat**
  - **Vulnerability**
  - **Likelihood**
  - **Impact**
- If a threat exists but there is no vulnerability, risk is lower
- If a vulnerability exists but there is no realistic threat, risk is lower
- Risk becomes serious when an important asset has a weakness that a real threat can exploit

## Likelihood and Impact
- **Likelihood:** How probable it is that a risk event may happen
- **Impact:** The damage or effect if the risk event happens
- Impact can be financial, operational, legal, reputational, or safety related

## Types of Risk
- **Inherent Risk:** Risk before security controls are applied
- **Residual Risk:** Risk remaining after controls are applied
- **Control Risk:** Risk that a control may fail or not work as expected
- **Risk Appetite:** Amount of risk an organization is willing to accept
- **Risk Tolerance:** Acceptable variation from the defined risk appetite

## Risk Response Strategies
- **Accept:** Acknowledge the risk and take no extra action beyond monitoring
- **Avoid:** Stop the risky activity completely
- **Transfer:** Move financial or operational responsibility to another party, such as insurance or outsourcing
- **Mitigate:** Reduce the likelihood or impact using controls

## Risk Register
- **Risk Register:** A document used to track identified risks and their treatment status
- Common fields include:
  - Risk ID
  - Asset affected
  - Threat or scenario
  - Vulnerability
  - Likelihood
  - Impact
  - Risk rating
  - Risk owner
  - Recommended treatment
  - Due date
  - Status

## Key Takeaway
- Risk management helps security teams decide what to fix first based on business impact, asset value, exposure, and likelihood.
