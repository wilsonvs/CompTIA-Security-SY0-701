# Risk Management

## Objectives

- Explain how risk is identified, measured, prioritized, and treated.
- Connect threats, vulnerabilities, assets, likelihood, impact, and controls.
- Understand how risk management supports security decisions and business continuity.

## Key Concepts

Risk is the possibility that a threat can exploit a vulnerability and create harm to an asset, system, process, or organization. A strong security program does not treat every weakness equally. It evaluates risk based on business impact, likelihood, exposure, and the value of the asset being protected.

Common risk terms:

- **Asset:** Anything valuable that needs protection, such as data, systems, users, facilities, applications, and business processes.
- **Threat:** Any circumstance or actor that can cause harm.
- **Vulnerability:** A weakness that can be exploited.
- **Likelihood:** Probability that a risk event may occur.
- **Impact:** Business, operational, financial, legal, or reputational effect if the risk occurs.
- **Inherent risk:** Risk before controls are applied.
- **Residual risk:** Risk that remains after controls are applied.
- **Risk appetite:** Amount of risk an organization is willing to accept.
- **Risk tolerance:** Acceptable variation around risk appetite.

## Risk Response Strategies

| Strategy | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Accept | Acknowledge the risk and take no further action beyond monitoring | Accepting low-impact risk from a non-critical internal tool |
| Avoid | Stop the activity that creates the risk | Removing an unsupported public-facing service |
| Transfer | Move financial or operational responsibility to another party | Cyber insurance or outsourced service agreements |
| Mitigate | Reduce likelihood or impact using controls | MFA, patching, backups, segmentation, monitoring |

## Risk Register Fields

A practical risk register should include:

- Risk ID
- Asset or process affected
- Threat scenario
- Vulnerability or weakness
- Likelihood
- Impact
- Overall risk rating
- Existing controls
- Recommended treatment
- Risk owner
- Due date or review date
- Status

## Practical Workflow

1. Identify assets and business processes.
2. Identify threats and vulnerabilities affecting those assets.
3. Estimate likelihood and impact.
4. Assign a risk rating.
5. Select a risk response strategy.
6. Document the decision in a risk register.
7. Track remediation or acceptance.
8. Reassess risk after changes, incidents, audits, or new threats.
